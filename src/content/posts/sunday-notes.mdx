three things from the week.

**one.** finally wrote [jonah](./jonah-brute-force-sql). two hours, including the writeup. this is the new bar — if i can't get a thing out the door in a weekend afternoon, it probably isn't a weekend project. it's a someday project. someday projects don't count.

**two.** keep catching myself [starting new things](./why-i-cant-finish-things) when i'm bored mid-way through the current thing. this week i started, and stopped:

- a scraper for a reddit community i don't even post in
- a vscode extension that would auto-generate commit messages
- a python script that would tag all my photos by vibe

none of these are ideas. they are procrastination costumed as ideas. i'm learning to tell the difference, slowly.

**three.** my friend m. called at 11pm on friday and we talked for an hour about nothing and it was the best thing that happened to me all week. reminder: good conversation is a renewable resource and i should stop treating it like a scheduled meeting.

---

quote i wrote down from a podcast i can't remember:

> a project is a conversation with yourself that you've agreed to have for a specific length of time.

i like this because it reframes finishing as honoring an agreement rather than as output. i don't need to finish the thing because it's valuable; i need to finish the thing because i told myself i would.
